What is a directory, and what is its relationship to files?
  A directory is the nesting of a folder in a hierarchical "tree" with a single root that branches off into related groups of content; the hierarchy also     describes the location of the files within the folder, the files are located in the parent directory which is under the parent to the child of directory. 
How are VCSs useful?
  VSCs (Version Control Systems) are useful because they act as a way to remember all the changes you have made; you can track changes over time, revert to a previous version of your work, as well as collaborate and share with associated parties.
